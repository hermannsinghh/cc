The Problem: Financial Literacy in Crisis

Most people get access to credit before receiving practical financial education. The data is alarming:

13%

of the Portuguese population masters basic financial concepts.

74%

of youth without financial literacy believe they know how to manage money.

59%

of the Portuguese have short-term debt.

⚠️ The Cost of Ignorance

In Portugal, young people have some of the highest rates of payment difficulties. This "overconfidence" significantly increases the risk of poorly informed financial decisions, fraud, and financial losses.

The Next Challenge: AI Will Spend Money For Us

Financial literacy was designed for a world where every purchase decision was made directly by a human. That world is changing.

Within the next decade, millions of people are expected to rely on AI assistants for financial decisions.

  • Booking travel
  • Purchasing subscriptions
  • Managing household expenses
  • Comparing loans and financial products
  • Negotiating services
  • Increasingly participating in purchasing decisions

These AI systems will increasingly interact with bank accounts, payment systems, credit products and digital wallets.

The challenge is no longer only:

"Can people make good financial decisions?"

The new challenge becomes:

"Can people and their AI agents make good financial decisions together?"

Portugal has an opportunity to become one of the first countries to prepare citizens for this future by creating an AI-powered financial literacy framework before AI-driven spending becomes mainstream.

Our Solution: Financial Driver's License

We don't tell users what they can't buy. We empower them with advanced financial optimization so they can buy smarter, avoid hidden monetary hurts, and leverage benefits they didn't know existed.

Why Start Now?

Most countries are discussing AI regulation after the technology arrives. Portugal can instead become a leader in proactive AI financial education.

This pilot would test not only whether AI can improve financial literacy today, but whether citizens can safely interact with future AI agents that will increasingly participate in purchasing and credit decisions.
